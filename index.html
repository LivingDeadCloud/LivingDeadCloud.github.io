TITANIC CHALLENGE
by Claudio Crema

Everything you need to know about the Kaggle Titanic challenge, presented by
Claudio Crema.

As probably everyone knows, the sinking of the Titanic was a tragic event that occurred in the early hours of 15 April 1912.
Of the 1309 passengers, 809 died. In this presentation I will analyze dataof the disaster.

In the first chart, I analyzed passengers' survival rate by sex.
As expected, Titanic male passengers were real gentlemen: 81% of them died, while 73% of women surived. I can hear them yelling "Ladies first!"
More in detail:
- 1st class survival rate = 62%
- 2nd class survival rate = 43%
- 3rd class survival rate = 26%

Tip: try the charts, they are interactive!

In the second chart, I analyzed passengers' survival rate by ticket class.
Needless to say, there are no surprises here either. First class passengers has a survival rate of 62%, which decreased to 43% for second class, and it got to a dramatic 26% for third class. They may have been gentlemen, but for sure they were classist as well!

More in detail:
- Males survival rate = 19%
- Females survival rate = 73%

Now ket's have a look at passengers survival rate by age.
I defined five age ranges (expressed in years):
- Babies: age < 3, survival rate = 63%
- Kids: 4 < age < 17, survival rate = 50%
- Adults: 18 < age < 40, survival rate = 38%
- Middle aged men: 41 < age < 60, survival rate = 42%
- Elderlies: age > 61, survival rate = 24%
Looks like the motto was "Ladies and babies first!"
